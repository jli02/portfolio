---the problem---

Oftentimes, websites for local businesses are not created by designers. The website for my family's restaurant, Hunan Garden, falls into this category. I decided to redesign Hunan Garden's website, not just because it could use some touch-ups with regards to usability and accessibility, but also because it's exciting to have the chance to give back in some way to my parents and this restaurant that raised me.

The contents of the current website are a little scattered around the page, there are duplicate buttons for ordering online, and the site is not optimized for mobile screens. From talking to my parents, I learned they also wanted a more modern looking site to attract customers.

---takeaways---

Even after coding the design, I feel a little conflicted about whether this is the "best" website design for the restaurant. I wish I was able to convey the cozy, welcoming environment of the restaurant through the design. How can we design websites with intention in order to convey a specific feeling or to capture the essence of what the website is for? Something I am still mulling over.

Through this project, I gained a greater understanding of the design process for a responsive website. It can be very time consuming!! Figuring out the low fidelity wireframes and high fidelity mockups definitely led to a smoother time coding. Responsivity and accessibility are big things to consider when developing any website.
