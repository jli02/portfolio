---the problem---

CUR8 is a startup that curates custom carbon removal portfolios according to customers' needs and goals. Currently, the process of generating a portfolio is done manually, and clients must consult with representatives from CUR8 for several months before making a purchase.

The company is hoping to streamline the purchasing process and has started designing a portfolio simulation tool. They asked us to continue on their design to create a purchasing flow so that users can buy the portfolios that they have generated.

---sketches---

We each designed two potential solutions to generate a wide variety of layout ideas. While we did these independently, we realized that many aspects of our flows overlapped, which helped us decide what would be most intuitive for our first iteration of wireframes.

---low-fidelity wireframes---

We then used these sketches to create our low-fidelity prototypes, which consisted of pages for a typical purchasing flow, like a review and confirmation page. We made additional considerations for buying a carbon removal portfolio, like including a page for users to request feedback from stakeholders.

---feedback---

We went through multiple iterations, implementing feedback from the CUR8 team, our professor, and peers. Main points that we focused on:

  • Giving users more details on specific carbon removal projects
  • Expanding the approval stage and how stakeholders can provide feedback
  • Making the final review page feel like a receipt

---prototypes---

---takeaways---

A purchasing flow is something we're all familiar with and seems rather straightforward, but we learned that there are actually very important considerations to make depending on the purpose of the product. Carbon removal portfolios are a complex product and must be thoughtfully considered before buying, so we had to design a flow with enough friction and information so that users are satisfied with what they are purchasing. We really enjoyed this project and learned a lot about the iterative design process!
